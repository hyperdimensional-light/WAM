Waves and Vortices in Continuous Media

Part 1: Waves and Vortices

In a continuous medium, such as air, you can have waves. Sound waves are waves of air.

Light is also a wave, but it is not a wave of air. Light is a wave of Ether.

A vortex can form in air. A tornado is a vortex of air. A vortex can also form in Ether.

This technology creates a vortex in the Ether.

Part 2: What is a vortex?

When a record spins around on a turntable, the outside edge spins faster than the middle.

When air spins around in a tornado, the middle spins around faster than the outer edge.

A tornado is a vortex. A record is not.

A vortex may look like a spiral, but that is just an artefact created by the circular motion of the medium. The medium spins around in circles. It does not spiral towards the center.

When you stir water in a bucket, it makes a vortex. The vortex makes a funnel shape. If you look at this funnel from the side, it has the form of a hyperbolic horn. The bottom point of this horn is infinitely far below the surface of the water.

A vortex is like a fire, in that they can both draw energy from their environment into themselves, thus growing larger and more powerful, and then able to draw even *more* energy into themselves. etc.

This process is called Growth, and is a characteristic of living things, such as trees.
