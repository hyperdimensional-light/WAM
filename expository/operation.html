Key Operational Parameters

A capacitor, charged to a high positive voltage, is discharged not to ground, but to a low positive voltage.

Ideally, this discharge is effected by a vacuum tube. Solid state switching devices (i.e, Transistors) are not recommended.

The capacitor is charged again to a high positive voltage, and the process is repeated.

The capacitor can be charged through a resonant charging choke.
In a pulse-forming network, the term used for this choke is "Resonant Charging Choke". See the Northstar High Voltage Pulsed Power Formulary.
